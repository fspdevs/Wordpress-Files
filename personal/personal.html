What does Par Funding do with your personal information?

Why? Financial companies collect and sometimes share personal information to underwrite funding services and prevent fraudulent activities on accounts. While Federal law gives consumers the right to limit some sharing, these laws don’t always extend to business transactions. This notice should help you to understand what we do.
What? The types of information we collect and share in processing & underwriting depends on the level of funding and service you apply for. This information can include:

  • Social Security number, birthdates, driver’s licenses and account balances
  • ACH transactions, business receipts personal and business credit histories
  • Banking account transactions, real estate holdings and other account information
How? All financial companies need to share customers' personal information to keep their everyday business running smoothly. In the section below, we list the reasons financial companies share customer information; the reasons Par Funding chooses to share; and whether you can limit this sharing.

What we do
How does Par Funding protect my personal information? To protect your personal information from unauthorized access and use, we use security measures that comply with federal law. These measures include computer safeguards, secured files and facilities.
How does Par Funding collect my personal information?

We collect your information, for example, when you

  • Apply for funding and give us your personal information
  • Provide underwriting and processing information about your business
  • Set up ACH payments and/or provide your banking information
We also collect information from others, such as credit bureaus, affiliates, or other companies.
Why can't I limit all sharing?

Federal law applies to consumer, not business transactions, and gives you the right to limit only:

  • sharing for affiliates' everyday consumer account purposes
  • affiliates from using your information to market to you
  • sharing for non-affiliates to market to you
State laws and individual companies may give you additional rights to limit sharing. See below for more on your rights under state law.
What happens when I use my personal credit and identity info to apply for business funding? Any person listed on an application for business funding agrees that they are doing so as a member/officer of the business. therefore, laws applying specifically to consumer transactions do not apply.
Definitions
Affiliates Companies related by contractual agreement, common ownership or control. They can be financial and nonfinancial companies.
  • Our affiliates include financial companies, as well as processing contractors, collection agents and independent sales organizations.
Non-affiliates Companies not related by common ownership or control. They can be financial and nonfinancial companies who subscribe to services.
  • Par Funding does not share with non-affiliates so they can market to you.
Joint marketing A formal agreement between non-affiliated financial companies that together market financial products or services to you.
  • Our joint marketing partner(s) include brokerage companies, a merchant servicing company and a credit card company.

EU Data Right to be Forgotten

Individuals in Europe may have certain data subject rights which may be subject to limitations and/or restrictions. These rights may include the right to: (i) request access to and rectification or erasure of their personal data; (ii) obtain restriction of processing or to object to processing of their personal data; and (iii) the right to data portability. If you wish to exercise one of the above mentioned rights, please call us at (855) 382-1908.
